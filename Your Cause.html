California Fires

Wildfire Damage

California naturally is prone to wildfires more than the East Coast because of the dry air and hotter tempertures, however the 2018 wildfire season was the most deadly season recorded in California. In total 8,527 fires occured burnind approximately 1,893,913 acres which is also the largest acreage recorded to be burned in a fire season.

Lives affected by the fires

Search and rescue crews continue to shift through debris from the fires and continue to find the remains of the victims. The death toll from this disaster now come to 42 not even including the 53 people who are missing who are assumed to be dead. The fires that tore thrpugh Nothern California destroyed at least 8,400 building and homes. The fires also destryed 7,500 structures throughout Northern California. The majority of houses that burned down were in the Santa Rosa area, however the number of homes that were destroyed in the mountainous area is 1,500. Lots of families lost their belongins, homes and more impotantly other family members. Neighborhoods were destroyed along with neighboorhood parks.

Prevention and raising awareness

The massive amount of damage and loss caused tons of orginizations to begin raising money for the victims and their families. One of these orgininizations is Pray for California. They are a non-profit ministry who raise awareness by accepting donations and praying for the families as a sign of respect and to make their community feel stronger. The DNR works to prevent wildfires by educating people about the dangers and burn restrictions.

Even though California and other states are prone to fires, there are ways of preventing them that we should talk about. Some prevention tactics are, making sure vehicles have operating spark arrestors, knowing the current wildfire risk of your area, keep flammable objects such as, lawnmowers, oil or gas cans and propane tanks at least 30 feet away from your house.These orginizations may not erase history but they do help to repair damage that has been done by the fires.
